### Recovered problem

Separate demand, incentives, constraints, and feedback loops, then collapse to one dominant upstream loop: discretionary reward-budget reallocation when compulsive occasions are removed. Apparel sizing and chronic-disease economics are downstream; they should not move until reward-loop metrics shift. ### Hidden constraint

Four loops: (1) reward-budget compression from occasion removal, (2) portion-size manufacturing response, (3) apparel identity refresh, (4) chronic-disease spend feedback. Assign leading metrics—occasion spend share, SKU calorie density, size-bucket returns, T2D pharmacy claims—and falsifiers per loop. Upstream loop (1) must lead with panel evidence; loops 3–4 lag ≥4 quarters or thesis mis-orders causality. It is tempered by Short-term GLP-1RAs improve regulation of emotional and cue-driven eating behaviors. That is a binary-ish control on 'food noise' rather than a smooth calorie dial: users skip entire occasion clusters (late-night pantry, social drinking calories, stress retail) without substituting calorie-for-calorie. Small pharmacological input → large basket deletion across unrelated SKUs. It is tempered by Shared pressure geometry: (1) pharmacological dampening of cue-driven consumption, (2) category-level volume collapse in the habitual good, (3) compensatory substitution in adjacent pleasures (alcohol, ultra-processed 'treat' positioning), (4) durable tail of lapsed users who re-enter the old habit loop after discontinuation. Food noise reduction behaves like craving decoupling—not a permanent taste change.

### Evaluation

#### Novelty +2

The four-loop collapse to a dominant reward-budget reallocation and the smoking-cessation geometry analogy add useful structure, but GLP-1-driven cross-category demand shocks and substitution dynamics are already widely discussed in consumer and health economics.

#### Grounding +4

It is well anchored in documented GLP-1 effects on cue-driven eating, plausible occasion-level basket deletion, and established post-cessation substitution and relapse patterns rather than relying on a permanent taste-change story.

#### Falsifiability +5

It names specific leading metrics, per-loop falsifiers, a quantified ≥8pp wallet-share threshold, and an explicit temporal ordering test where downstream loops must lag upstream movement by at least four quarters.

#### Cost-efficiency +3

The thesis is mostly testable with existing household panels, retail SKU data, returns, and pharmacy claims, but tracking four loops with quarterly lead-lag validation still imposes non-trivial analytical overhead.

#### Relevance +5

It directly operationalizes the appetite-shock thesis across food volume, snacks, alcohol, apparel, and chronic-disease economics with an explore-and-proof gate that prioritizes upstream spend reallocation before downstream indicators.
