### Implications

- Mechanism: The convenience flag is not generic incorporation shopping—it optimizes only what a permissive AV registry can: (1) Texas DPS commercial deployment permit holder and designated operator of record, (2) insurance certificate filing situs tied to that permit, and (3) California DMV/NHTSA autonomous-vehicle noncompliance notice recipient of record. California triggers—safety-driver absence events, disengagement-report gaps, ODD boundary violations—escalate on the CA notice channel while Texas authorization remains valid for miles never filed there. Falsifiable metric: Flag Divergence Ratio (FDR) = California miles operated ÷ California notices acknowledged, divided by Texas miles operated ÷ Texas authorization renewals filed; FDR > 2.5 sustained two quarters proves filed jurisdiction diverged from citation venue. Manchester-style stops where the presenting party is not the filed operator are the measurement event, not the optimization target. It is tempered by Shared structure → shared remedy: 'citation domicile parity'—any traffic stop in state S must resolve against the registry where the vehicle's commercial AV authority was filed unless S issues a superseding noncompliance notice already indexed. Officer queries one parity API; fleet cannot route around California notices by Texas filing because parity rule pulls notices into stop resolution. Operator chokepoint threshold governs only the live identity handoff, not which registry's sanctions apply. It is tempered by Import port-state control doctrine: require any AV operating in a host state to carry a real-time 'port clearance packet'—filing jurisdiction, authorized operator-of-record, remote supervisor chain, and noncompliance notice status—analogous to IMO-mandated vessel identification. Host-state officers get a single verification surface instead of guessing which registry wins. Operator Chokepoint Threshold applies at the packet handoff: below threshold, vehicle must pull over and present machine-readable authority before officer approaches.
