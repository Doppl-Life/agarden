### Claim

Separate demand, incentives, constraints: even when observed crashes don't fall on schedule, three incentives preserve accident-economy cash flows—(1) deferred reserve releases on disputed AV attribution, (2) litigation-funnel intake fees on severity-dispute carry, (3) cat-bond coupon continuity while PCS triggers remain unbreached. The non-obvious dependent the synopsis treats as downstream of crash volume is legal-tech ADR capital rotation, which sustains dispute throughput without new collisions. The child imports the secondary constraint from Twin Cliffs: L4 Mile-Share Threshold and Severity-Mix Inversion.

### Implications

- Mechanism: Demand for risk-transfer paper stays alive via coupon mechanics tied to index triggers, not realized losses. Incentives: insurers delay reserve releases when fault is disputed; PI firms monetize severity inflation on fewer cases; legal-tech ADR platforms earn per-dispute fees replacing collision intake. Measurable loop break: when driverless miles exceed 30% of fleet miles in a served market AND ADR dispute fees per mile fall below legacy litigation intake revenue per mile, the first incentive reverses—reserve releases accelerate and combined ratios spike before raw crash counts move. It is tempered by Cliff one metric: citation revenue per vehicle-mile in AV-corridor MSAs (falsifier: revenue flat despite >25% robotaxi share). Cliff two metric: BI severity mix ratio (product-liability vs. driver-negligence docket share) (falsifier: mix unchanged despite J3161 adoption). Single-threshold story fails because fiscal and insurance repricing decouple across cliffs. Actor between cliffs: fleet surplus-lines brokers holding basis risk against frequency tables after cliff one but before cliff two.
