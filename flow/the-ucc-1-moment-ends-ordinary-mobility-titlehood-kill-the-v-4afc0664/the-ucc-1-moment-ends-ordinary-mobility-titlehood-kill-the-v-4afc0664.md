### Recovered problem

VIN-continuity drag is not generic DMV friction—it is the legal requirement that ordinary mobility pass through UCC-1 lien filing and title transfer. A small mechanism (trip account without transferable title event per trip) collapses the multi-product stack faster than autonomy alone. The child imports the secondary constraint from Kill the VIN-as-Identity Loop and Ownership Unravels Fast / The Real Customer Is the Lender-Insurer Stack, Not the Driver fusion.

### Hidden constraint

Quantified VIN-continuity friction: median time-to-clear-title on private-party sale runs 2–6 weeks with lien release dependency (state DMV + lender workflow); each ownership event triggers re-underwriting, registration fee ($200–$800 state-dependent), and insurance policy rewrite. Separate from bureaucracy: the subjecthood requirement itself. Exact moment title stops being necessary: when >60% of a household's annual trips can be fulfilled by a pooled VIN whose liability sits with operator MCO, not household balance sheet—commute + airport + evening leisure inside service polygon. Breakthrough intervention: operator-held fleet VIN with household mobility pass (no security interest)—one product design choice, not nationwide regulatory rewrite. It is tempered by Today one VIN anchors a bundle: secured loan, insurer rating, registration, garage sizing, and trade-in equity math. A platform that lets the same physical robot rotate across fleet balance sheets (or subscriber minutes) without transferring household liability breaks the 'largest stupid purchase' loop at the paperwork layer, not the steering wheel. It is tempered by Invert the stated problem: Americans do not organize life around mobility—they organize it around encumbered metal that must stay insured, registered, and geographically proximate so monthly payment streams and municipal parking regimes remain collectible. Robotaxi and supervised FSD attack utilization, not the balance-sheet identity of the car as the largest levered purchase in the household budget.

### Falsifier

Bounds breakthrough to a single legal-product hinge (operator liability shell) with a trip-share threshold, avoiding generic 'regulation is slow' narratives. + Small legal/fintech mechanism (fleet-title pooling or per-mile liability shell) yields outsized behavioral shift: households stop sizing life around 'my car nearby' because no single asset must be defended, maintained, and refinanced. + Reframes autonomy competition from 'cheaper rides' to 'who loses when the car stops being a financed depreciating asset with mandatory carry costs'—surfacing lenders, insurers, dealers, and cities as the neglected stakeholders whose incentives explain why ownership persists even when driving utility could be unbundled.
