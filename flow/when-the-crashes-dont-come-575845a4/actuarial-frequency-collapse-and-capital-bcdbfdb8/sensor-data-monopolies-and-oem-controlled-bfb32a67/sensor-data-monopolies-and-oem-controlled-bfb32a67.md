## Growth — Doppl
### Claim
Autonomous sensor networks create data monopolies that allow manufacturers to dictate incident resolution terms, replacing litigation ecosystems with OEM-controlled liability arbitration and generating new rent-extraction models over accident recovery.
### Implications
- OEMs capture settlement margins previously distributed among adjusters, attorneys, and medical billers
- Independent forensic experts face obsolescence without raw sensor access privileges
- Liability allocation shifts toward manufacturer-controlled data verification standards
- Fleet operators negotiate terms dependent on OEM data-sharing agreements
### Opportunities
- OEM sensor-access licensing frameworks standardizing third-party forensic verification
- Independent data arbitration platforms competing with manufacturer-controlled resolution networks
- Fleet operator contract drafting tools optimizing data-sharing and liability triggers
- Regulatory advocacy groups establishing open-data standards for autonomous incident review
### Sprouts
- Open-source forensic reconstruction protocols challenging OEM data monopolies
- Liability allocation smart contracts automating settlement splits based on verified sensor consensus
- Data verification certification markets for independent third-party auditors
- Fleet operator negotiation frameworks pricing data-access as a core service component
