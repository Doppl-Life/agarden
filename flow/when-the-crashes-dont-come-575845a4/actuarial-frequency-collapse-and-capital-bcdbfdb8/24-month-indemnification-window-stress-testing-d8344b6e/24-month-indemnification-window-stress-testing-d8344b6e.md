## Growth — Doppl
### Claim
Mandating a 24-month liability transfer window forces OEMs, fleet operators, and insurers to structurally align warranty terms, insurance deductibles, and operational SLAs around rapid indemnification triggers rather than static depreciation schedules.
### Implications
- Warranty periods compress as manufacturers accelerate risk-transfer to insurers
- Fleet operators face accelerated depreciation modeling without traditional crash recovery offsets
- Insurance deductibles shift from fixed amounts to percentage-based liability thresholds
- Contractual indemnification clauses become the primary risk-allocation mechanism across all stakeholders
### Opportunities
- Warranty restructuring advisory services optimizing 24-month liability transfer triggers
- Fleet contract drafting platforms automating indemnification SLA alignment with deployment velocity
- Insurance deductible modeling tools pricing percentage-based liability thresholds for autonomous lines
- Cross-party risk-allocation frameworks standardizing indemnification windows across OEMs, fleets, and insurers
### Sprouts
- Dynamic warranty termination protocols tied to real-time software maturity metrics
- Fleet operator risk-cash flow hedging products aligned with indemnification trigger cycles
- Regulatory frameworks requiring standardized liability-transfer disclosure in autonomy service agreements
- Contract automation platforms mapping multi-party indemnification dependencies
