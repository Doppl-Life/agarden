### Case study · synopsis

Usage-based autonomy pricing decouples mobility costs from vehicle ownership and traditional credit scoring, disrupting consumer lending portfolios and forcing financial institutions to redesign risk models around behavioral telemetry.

## Growth — Problem recovery
### Surface complaint
Credit bureaus lack the granularity to assess mobility risk accurately.
### Deleted assumption
Private entities should control and profit from consumer behavioral data.
### Hidden variable
Centralized data monopolies create systemic fragility when usage patterns shift rapidly.
### Actual problem
Monopolized behavioral data prevents transparent, competitive pricing of mobility solvency.
### Candidate response
Establish sovereign mobility data trusts that aggregate, anonymize, and license telemetry for public risk modeling.
