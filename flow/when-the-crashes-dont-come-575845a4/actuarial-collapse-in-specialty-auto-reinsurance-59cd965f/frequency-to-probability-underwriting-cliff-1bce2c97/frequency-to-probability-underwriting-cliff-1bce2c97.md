## Growth — Doppl

### Claim

When crash frequency drops below actuarial thresholds, capital markets face immediate basis risk because insurance captives and reinsurance treaties are priced on historical loss ratios, not forward-looking probability distributions.

### Implications

- Specialty auto captives must renegotiate treaty triggers or face insolvency as claimed losses fall faster than premium reserves
- Traditional P&amp;C insurers will misprice autonomy risk by relying on stale human-error baselines
- Capital allocation shifts from claims reserving to telemetry validation infrastructure

### Opportunities

- Build probability-weighted loss models that accept zero-frequency periods as valid data points
- Create transitional reinsurance wrappers that bridge historical ratio pricing with real-time AV safety confidence intervals
- Develop captive management firms specializing in autonomy-era risk capital deployment

### Sprouts

- NAIC captive registration delays correlate with crash-volume compression timelines
- Reinsurer financial statements show growing unearned premium reserves tied to legacy auto lines
