## Growth — Doppl
### Claim
Regulatory asymmetry across states and nations creates geographic arbitrage opportunities that artificially deflate loss ratios, triggering adverse selection in regulated captive markets and forcing a fragmentation of liability assignment.
### Implications
- Fleet operators will route incident data through low-liability jurisdictions to maintain favorable actuarial profiles
- Regulated markets face capital flight as risk migrates to unverified testing zones
- Federal harmonization becomes economically urgent to prevent shadow litigation accumulation
### Opportunities
- Build cross-jurisdictional liability standardization platforms for AV fleet operators
- Develop regulatory compliance oracles that auto-route incident data through standardized adjudication pipelines
- Create interstate risk-sharing agreements that neutralize arbitrage incentives
### Sprouts
- Texas Senate Bill 26 and California DMV tester list show divergent liability assignment standards
- EU AI Act mandates strict product-liability frameworks while US NHTSA relies on voluntary safety plans
- AV fleet expansion patterns correlate with states offering litigation caps or testing waivers
