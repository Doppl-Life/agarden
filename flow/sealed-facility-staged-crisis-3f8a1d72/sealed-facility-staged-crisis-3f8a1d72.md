# Sealed Facility / Staged Crisis

prev_id: null

## Context

A coordinated criminal team seizes a sealed, high-value corporate facility under the cover of a political hostage crisis. The team is armed, organized, and operating on a precise internal timeline. Their stated demands — political, ideological — fit a well-understood threat profile. Official response mobilizes immediately: law enforcement, professional negotiators, and federal authorities escalate through standard protocols.

Everything inside the facility is locked down. One person is not accounted for. A civilian professional who happened to be inside at the moment of the seizure is now physically present, undetected, and unknown to all parties — the response commanders, the negotiators, and the criminal team itself.

The official response escalates correctly by every standard measure: perimeter secured, negotiators engaged, federal jurisdiction assumed, internal access systematically ceded in exchange for hostage cooperation. None of it neutralizes the threat. The criminal plan advances in step with the official response, as if the two are in coordination.

The civilian, with no chain of command and no resources beyond what the building contains, begins acting independently.

## Synopsis

A criminal team stages a political hostage crisis inside a sealed corporate facility to conceal a financial theft; the official response escalates through every standard protocol while the civilian insider — unknown to all parties — acts alone.
