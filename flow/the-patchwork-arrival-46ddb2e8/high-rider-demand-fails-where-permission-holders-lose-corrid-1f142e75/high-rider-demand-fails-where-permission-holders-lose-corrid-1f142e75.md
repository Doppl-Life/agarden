### Recovered problem

Patchwork arrival looks like a technology problem only if you delete the institutional payoff. Latent rider demand and permission-holder incentive diverge: demand signals peak in corridors where insurers, municipalities, and captives still earn on human-error frequency and fine revenue. The child imports the secondary constraint from Corridors Freeze at the Interconnection–Treasury Double Gate.

### Hidden constraint

Separate demand from incentive with one signal each. Demand signal: stated willingness-to-use autonomous ride-hail in corridor surveys and app waitlists. Institutional payoff signal: net retained margin on human-error-priced fleet paper plus municipal fine-and-fee receipts per vehicle-mile. Deleted assumption: that demand conversion is technology-limited. Falsifier: corridors with top-quartile demand but bottom-quartile permission-holder payoff show zero conversion growth over 4 quarters while adjacent payoff-favorable corridors scale—proving block is incentive, not capability. It is tempered by Preserve parent mechanisms separately, then synthesize. Breakout leg: time-to-autonomous-energize = median days from insurer liability-study filing to operations sign-off; breaks first on study-cost pass-through. Blindside leg: municipal treasurer veto when fine-revenue loss projects >3% of general-fund operating revenue without bonded replacement. Compatibility test: corridors that clear insurer queue but fail treasury pre-commitment still show zero rollout—patchwork is the intersection, not either gate alone. Synthesis: the silent veto is fiscal; the silent queue is liability study; together they explain why some metros energize like solar interconnections and others re-litigate every electron and every crash.

### Falsifier

Splits latent demand from permission-holder payoff, names the deleted tech-limit assumption, and adds a falsifier for high-demand/low-conversion corridors blocked by institutional economics. + Mutates the generation-0 survivor by fusing interconnection queue metrics with falsifiable municipal fiscal thresholds into a double-gate model that survives critic pressure on both parent frames.
