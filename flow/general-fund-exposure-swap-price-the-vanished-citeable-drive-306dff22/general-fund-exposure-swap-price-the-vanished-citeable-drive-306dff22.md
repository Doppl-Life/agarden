### Implications

- Mechanism: Treat municipal fine-revenue erosion (>40% in AV-pilot corridors) as a standardized credit event—analogous to how fleet risk paper uses crash-frequency triggers—so cities can hedge general-fund exposure before bond covenants breach. Municipal issuers publish corridor-level citeable-driver indices; insurers and reinsurers write triggers on fine-receipt shortfalls, converting an unbudgeted cliff into a priced, transferable obligation. It is tempered by Mandate municipal fine-revenue attestation—disaggregating citation, forfeiture, and court-fee lines from discretionary revenue—in bond disclosures and annual comprehensive financial reports. Only after attestation can replacement instruments (wear charges, credit-event hedges) be sized correctly. The constraint injection blocks premature fleet-operator subject-transfer solutions that ignore the city's actual revenue geometry. It is tempered by Build a municipal exposure ladder from CAFR and court-audit lines: (1) moving-violation fines, (2) court-assessment and filing fees tied to driver citations, (3) municipal forfeiture-share allocations, (4) registration and surcharge fees that require a cited operator. Pilot AV-corridor cities already show fine revenue down >40%; triage orders jurisdictions by the share of unrestricted general-fund balance each line carries. The four-demands subject-transfer fork is explicit: each demand loses its citeable-driver on-ramp and no fleet account absorbs the receipt without charter or state-law reassignment—dollars land as a general-fund gap, not a fleet compliance line item.

### Evaluation

#### Novelty +3

Reframing vanished citeable drivers as a hedgeable municipal credit event—not fleet liability—is a genuine stakeholder inversion, though it extends familiar revenue-risk and trigger-insurance patterns rather than inventing a new fiscal category.

#### Grounding +2

CAFR lines, bond covenants, and court-fee disaggregation anchor the mechanism in real municipal finance, but headline claims (>40% corridor drops, 40–60% undershoot, 12–24 month runway) are asserted without cited pilots or issuer precedents in the summary.

#### Falsifiability +3

Corridor citeable-driver indices, attested receipt ladders, and covenant-breach timing against hedge payouts define measurable tests, even if insurer appetite and trigger calibration remain open empirical questions.

#### Cost-efficiency +1

Attestation mandates, exposure ladders, triage, and bespoke reinsurance add substantial upfront friction for cities that may be too small or too late to access liquid hedge markets, partially offset only if cliff avoidance is real and large.

#### Relevance +5

It directly answers the case brief—municipal general-fund exposure when citeable drivers disappear—by naming the treasurer, ranking receipt lines, and blocking fleet-operator subject-transfer that misplaces the binding constraint.
