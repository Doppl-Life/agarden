# The Dynamic Stasis Protocol

prev_id: [[the-time-warp-event-protocol-5b0de374]]

## Trace
### Case study · synopsis

Implementing a party schedule that incorporates highly regulated, visible 'Protocol Windows' where *all* activity is deliberately paused for 15-minute periods. These windows are marketed as unique experiences (e.g., astronomical viewing, mandatory collective relaxation) and function by establishing an expected pattern of total low-activity stillness, rendering the drone’s primary value—capturing candid high-action moments—null by making the target asset *unphotographable* in bursts.

## Growth — Problem recovery
### Surface complaint
Mandatory Protocol Windows nullify high-action candid moments by enforcing stillness.
### Deleted assumption
That all periods of low activity must be uniform, passive, and identical in structure.
### Hidden variable
The novelty derived from the *variability* of constraint itself.
### Actual problem
The 'Protocol Window' is too monolithic; its predictability leads to boredom and content fatigue, even if it successfully stops action.
### Candidate response
Replace single 15-minute windows with a sequence of rapidly alternating, micro-constrained periods (e.g., 3 minutes: mandated silence/sound recording; 2 minutes: mandatory eye contact with stranger; 4 minutes: observing only one color). The high variability increases the perceived novelty and difficulty of compliance.
