## Growth — Problem recovery
### Surface complaint
Paparazzi drones can film open decks and capture personal scandal photos.
### Deleted assumption
That the content of the photo is the only thing that matters to the paparazzi.
### Hidden variable
The commercial value of a photograph, which depends on novelty, context, and emotional impact.
### Actual problem
Implementing real-time, automated detection and modification of 'high-value' visual moments (e.g., specific gestures, rare interactions) that are most likely to be monetized by the paparazzi.
### Candidate response
Deploy a passive internal monitoring system using AI trained on deepfake/scandal metrics. When the system detects an interaction or pose flagged as high-potential scandal material, it triggers localized, non-visible interventions (e.g., targeted ambient soundscapes, strategic lighting shifts) that subtly interrupt the moment—not by separating people, but by changing their emotional state or focus just enough that the *memory* of the scene is altered, making any subsequent photo less potent.
