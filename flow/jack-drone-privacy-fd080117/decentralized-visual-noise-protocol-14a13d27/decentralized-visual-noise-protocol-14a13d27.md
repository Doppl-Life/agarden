## Growth — Problem recovery
### Surface complaint
Paparazzi drones can film open decks and capture personal scandal photos.
### Deleted assumption
That the yacht must maintain a single, consistent visual identity when viewed from afar.
### Hidden variable
The limited capacity of human attention to process highly varied, contradictory stimuli simultaneously.
### Actual problem
Creating an intentional, complex optical illusion where multiple sources of light, movement, and reflective surfaces constantly contradict each other's perceived location or nature, making the yacht appear geometrically unstable from altitude.
### Candidate response
Install modular, retractable panels and decorative elements across the entire superstructure. These elements are treated with highly specialized, low-power electrochromic films that can rapidly shift their reflectivity (e.g., alternating between mirror finish, matte black, and iridescent pearl). When viewed from above, these constantly shifting reflective surfaces create a chaotic, non-linear visual profile—a 'visual stutter'—that overwhelms the drone camera’s ability to map solid geometry or stable points of interest.
### Skin in the Game
- Advanced Materials Scientists
- Architectural Lighting Designers
- Electrochromic Film Manufacturers
### Sprouts
- Integrating panels with decorative lighting arrays for maximum effect.
- Developing panel control systems that mimic natural, chaotic movement (e.g., wave reflection).
