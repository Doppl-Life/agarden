# Polymath: The Behavioral Economics Leak

prev_id: [[controlled-data-garbage-release-protocol-a8b22a49]]

## Trace
### Case study · synopsis

In preparation for the party, the yacht strategically leaks large volumes of highly benign, but identifiable, operational data onto secure (but non-secure) public data feeds/forums and local media channels. This data pertains to generalized maritime operations that are either overly detailed or slightly inaccurate, creating a persistent, low-level digital background narrative about the yacht's activities that is constantly being analyzed by *other* people (media, reporters). The goal is to generate so much predictable, trivial 'data noise' about the boat that any single scandalous photo becomes statistically irrelevant.

## Growth — Problem recovery
### Surface complaint
We need to leak so much benign data that a scandal becomes noise.
### Deleted assumption
That media attention is driven by objective truth or technical complexity.
### Hidden variable
The predictable human reaction to perceived scarcity, risk, and social confirmation bias (FOMO/FOGO).
### Actual problem
Manipulating the public's emotional response to data leaks, making them interpret neutral facts through a lens of manufactured alarm.
### Candidate response
We will leak data that is entirely benign but strategically formatted to mimic signals of *risk* or *scarcity*. For example, releasing consumption rates paired with highly sensitive-sounding metrics (e.g., 'Resource X depletion rate approaching T-minus 72 hours') without ever defining Resource X. This triggers alarmist behavior and speculation in the public domain before any scandal can take root.
### Skin in the Game
- A behavioral economist consultant for leak framing.
- Data visualizations that use color, urgency markers, and 'critical' language.
### Sprouts
- Developing a library of high-impact, low-specificity warning metrics.
- Targeting social media influencers who specialize in alarming predictions.
