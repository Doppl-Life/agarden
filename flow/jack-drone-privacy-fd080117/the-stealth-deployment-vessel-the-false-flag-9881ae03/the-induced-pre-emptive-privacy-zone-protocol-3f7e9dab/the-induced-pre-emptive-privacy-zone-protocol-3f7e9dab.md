## Growth — Doppl
### Claim
Before guests arrive, the captain files an advisory with local authorities and private patrol services declaring the yacht's temporary location as a mandatory 'Research/Monitoring Isolation Zone' due to unannounced scientific testing or filming. This grants legitimate, immediate grounds for requiring non-essential vessels (and therefore paparazzi drones) to maintain extreme distance.
### Implications
- Losses: Requires proactive networking with local law enforcement and the willingness to assume a high-level operational status; potentially restricting desired guest approach/exit points.
- Wins: The 'exclusion' is based on plausible, bureaucratic necessity (e.g., conservation study, survey), not security fear. This legally forces external observation at arm's length.
### Opportunities
- Develop a rotating list of scientifically credible, non-disruptive scientific 'excuses' for varying locations and times.
- Collaborate with university marine biology departments to legitimize the claim via temporary project funding.
- Use the regulatory friction created by this protocol as its primary defense.
### Sprouts
- Deploying visible, high-quality 'scientific' signage that appears professionally sanctioned (e.g., required survey markers).
- Requiring pre-clearance and temporary exclusion zones from local port management based on pseudo-scientific necessity.
