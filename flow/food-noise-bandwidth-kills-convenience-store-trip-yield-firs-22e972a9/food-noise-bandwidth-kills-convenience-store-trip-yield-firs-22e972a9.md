### Hidden constraint

Define bandwidth freed behaviorally: fewer spontaneous store entries, fewer between-meal purchases, fewer 'already here' add-ons. Freed bandwidth reallocates to planned nutrition and non-food cognition (scheduling, errands consolidation), not abstinence. The first retail surface to lose yield is high-frequency, low-planning convenience and front-of-store grab zones—not grocery staples or premium protein. It is tempered by Demand = f(biological need, reward salience, habit infrastructure, price, coverage). GLP-1RAs reduce appetite and hunger acutely and improve behavioral regulation short term, but do not erase occasion-based eating or brand loyalty. Feedback loop: weight loss → apparel/size shift → identity reinforcement → adherence; discontinuation → appetite rebound → category reversion. Incentives split: payers save on comorbidities while CPG fights for 'GLP-1-friendly' positioning—neither side optimizes total calorie minimization. It is tempered by GLP-1RAs improve short-term regulation of eating behaviors, especially emotional and cue-driven intake, decoupling consumption from stress and environment. That shifts demand from high-frequency, low-utility SKUs (snacks, delivery fees, impulse alcohol) toward fewer, deliberate meals and non-food categories (fitness, apparel resize, sleep/health services). Economic consequence scales through decision-frequency reduction, not grams of protein avoided.

### Falsifier

Maintainer cohorts should show trip-count and front-end impulse yield falling weeks before premium-protein or meal-kit spend rises—falsifier if category trade-up precedes occasion-count decline. + Predicts stable aggregate food spend with mix rotation (protein-forward, smaller portions, premium single-serve) and delayed chronic-disease cost curves tied to adherent subpopulation, not headline adoption rates. + Identifies snack/immediate-consumption channels and 'decision-tax' categories as first casualties; claims durable wallet share migration even at modest average calorie reduction.

#### Novelty +3

GLP-1 retail disruption is already widely discussed, but reframing harm as decision-frequency and cognitive-bandwidth loss—with convenience impulse yield collapsing before visible category trade-up—is a sharper, less generic causal ordering.

#### Grounding +3

It aligns with documented GLP-1 effects on appetite and cue-driven eating and with early snack/convenience weakness signals, but the bandwidth mechanism and precise retail-surface sequencing remain behavioral extrapolation rather than established evidence.

#### Falsifiability +4

The maintainer-cohort prediction that trip count and front-end impulse yield fall before premium-protein or meal-kit spend rises is a clear, time-ordered falsifier testable on panel retail and adherence-linked cohorts.

#### Cost-efficiency +3
