### Claim

Invert the traffic-stop frame: the binding actor is the municipal enforcement budget that citation volume replenishes, not the human behind the wheel. ### Implications

- Mechanism: Treat each patrol corridor as a revenue-yield map tied to enforcement headcount and equipment spend. When AV share crosses a corridor threshold, trigger a fiscal cliff review that separates safety-demand spend from fine-dependent enforcement spend. Redirect patrol from citation-yield maximization to collision-density targeting funded by an explicit enforcement line item, not floating fine receipts. It is tempered by Legislate a four-line municipal budget: (1) safety outcomes funded by collision metrics, (2) fiscal continuity funded by wear-based usage charges, (3) identity verification funded as compliance infrastructure, (4) zero coupling between lines 1–3 and citation receipts. Patrol KPIs track collision density only; fine revenue is reported but cannot backfill enforcement headcount. It is tempered by Map municipal general-fund and special-revenue ledgers where traffic-fine deposits cross-walk to court operations, warrant-service contracts, and DMV compliance staffing — jurisdictions already showing >40% fine-revenue drops in AV-pilot corridors. Keep the enforcement-budget frame distinct from generic fine-loss by naming which patrol deployments were citation-yield-gated versus safety-KPI-gated. Trace the second budget hole: when the compliance substrate rail collapses, identity proof and warrant throughput break before collision-reduction demand does.
- Claimed delta: Surfaces the hidden budget stakeholder before AV corridors erase the fine-revenue cliff (>40% drops in pilot cities), preventing enforcement collapse that safety demand alone cannot fund. + Breaks the citation-volume → enforcement-funding feedback loop while preserving all three surviving demands; addresses the compliance substrate the fine revenue previously funded without renaming the safety mission. + Surfaces the neglected stakeholder — compliance payroll capitalized by citation volume — and predicts underfunded identity and warrant workflows arrive before any AV safety dividend, giving a fiscal early-warning signal bond markets miss.
