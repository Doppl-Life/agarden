# Containment before attribution

prev_id: [[the-insider-outside-the-recognition-channel-2ca2350c]]

## Trace

### Case study · synopsis

A criminal team stages a political hostage crisis inside a sealed corporate facility to conceal a financial theft; the official response escalates through every standard protocol while the civilian insider — unknown to all parties — acts alone.

### Problem recovery · synopsis

The Insider Outside the Recognition Channel

## Discovery

### Finding 1

Rapid structural change leaves insider/outsider doctrines misaligned with who actually holds local knowledge. → field: [[insider-outsider-doctrine]]

### Finding 2

Insider-threat defense fails upstream at identification, before any detection pipeline can engage. → field: [[identification-bottleneck]]

### Finding 3

Threat-recognition models structurally exclude unintentional insiders whose risk is inaction, not adversarial intent. → field: [[unintentional-insider-gap]]

## Growth — Doppl

### Claim

Security posture that treats identification as downstream of containment—not upstream of any response—closes the gap where accurate anonymous actors remain outside every institutional detection channel by definition.

### Implications

- Anonymous insiders lose the default protection of pre-identification invisibility
- Detection-first vendors and attribution-dependent monitoring workflows lose as the primary control plane
- Zero-trust and capability-based access architectures gain as the first response layer

### Opportunities

- Behavioral containment policies that activate on environmental signals without actor identity
- Capability-scoped access controls that limit blast radius regardless of insider classification
- Anonymous risk scoring that triggers isolation before attribution completes

### Evaluation

#### Novelty +2

Contain-first-then-attribute is established IR doctrine; the recognition-channel reframing adds angle more than mechanism.

#### Grounding +4

Sound in incident-response and assume-breach practice: unidentified actors outside channels still warrant immediate isolation.

#### Falsifiability +3

Policy and tabletop comparisons can measure time-to-containment and harm when attribution gates response.

#### Cost-efficiency +2

Cheaper than prolonged exposure from attribution delays, though broader containment still carries operational friction.

#### Relevance +4

Directly answers unrecognized insiders by making response independent of institutional identification.
