### Hidden constraint

Separate layers: mobility demand (miles), earnings incentives (OEM/fleet/platform on utilization), hard constraints (legal subject of driving, franchise title law, floorplan credit), and feedback loops (telematics claims reduction → premium compression → captive lease spread tightening → residual curve revision). Autonomy removes the reason to own when the legal subject of everyday miles shifts to fleet registrant or OEM warranty bearer—signaled by state permission for driverless deployment plus insurer assumption of OEM direct settlement. Three paths: pure-service eliminates household risk anchor; owned-income-asset retitles household as lessor; hybrid keep-buying leaves household as mispriced risk bearer on a depreciating asset markets will soon treat as revenue infrastructure. It is tempered by Decompose the stated problem into four load-bearing layers: floorplan and ABS demand tied to unit velocity; insurer liability keyed to driver-as-subject; claims reconstruction via adjusters from incomplete testimony; municipal revenue from driver violations. Autonomy attacks all four simultaneously by removing the human driver as the pricing unit. It is tempered by Start from the failure mode: if private autonomous car replacement rates stay near current levels while fleet miles remain marginal, the consumer-ownership thesis is wrong but the frequency-trigger collapse is still real. Specialty captives and fleet-liability derivatives strand first; households discover the unwind only when premiums, residuals, or trade-in values gap against repriced fleet risk.
