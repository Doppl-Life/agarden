# Cognitive State Mapping &amp; Predictive Avoidance

prev_id: [[psycho-acoustic-broadcast-frequency-interference-324a95a7]]

## Trace
### Case study · synopsis

Utilizing a low-power, directional acoustic transmitter that broadcasts specific, ultra-low frequency (infrasound) tones below the threshold of conscious hearing but known to affect human equilibrium and peripheral vision. This causes subtle disorientation or 'visual flicker' in drone camera lenses operating on optical stabilization algorithms.

## Growth — Problem recovery
### Surface complaint
Infrasound causes visual flicker in drone optical stabilization.
### Deleted assumption
That technical fixes (sensors, algorithms) are sufficient; the human element remains a variable.
### Hidden variable
The synergistic interaction between observed physical instability and the operator's cognitive load/fatigue state.
### Actual problem
System failure due to poor operational decision-making under low-level psycho-acoustic stress, leading to critical error rates.
### Candidate response
Integrate a module that continuously monitors human operators (via heart rate variability, eye tracking, and voice analysis) for signs of disorientation or cognitive strain correlated with the interference. When a high risk score is reached, the system autonomously mandates operational pauses, suggests mission profile changes, or takes temporary manual control to maintain safety.
### Skin in the Game
- Advanced physiological monitoring hardware (wearables)
- AI-driven cognitive load assessment models
- Human factors engineering protocols for high-stress environments.
### Sprouts
- Automated crew resource management in complex machinery.
- Preemptive warning systems for human operators entering dangerous zones.
