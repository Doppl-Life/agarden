# Media Immunity Treaty Protocol (MITP)

prev_id: [[the-induced-pre-emptive-privacy-zone-protocol-b6ce1582]]

## Trace
### Case study · synopsis

Before guests arrive, the captain files an advisory with local authorities and private patrol services declaring the yacht's temporary location as a mandatory 'Research/Monitoring Isolation Zone' due to unannounced scientific testing or filming. This grants legitimate, immediate grounds for requiring non-essential vessels (and therefore paparazzi drones) to maintain extreme distance.

## Growth — Problem recovery
### Surface complaint
Paparazzi drones and non-essential vessels infringe upon the yacht's private space, disrupting privacy.
### Deleted assumption
That local law enforcement is the primary enforcer of boundaries; that media actors fear legal action more than public backlash.
### Hidden variable
The leverage of reputation damage via pre-emptive contractual agreements with third parties (media/PR firms).
### Actual problem
Lack of a mechanism to make unauthorized surveillance legally and financially toxic *before* the event occurs, making it irrational for the media to attempt intrusion.
### Candidate response
Before arrival, engage specialized legal counsel to draft and pre-circulate a non-disclosure/non-intrusion agreement framework. This is not presented to authorities, but offered as an 'operational warning' to key press agencies and drone service providers: any violation will result in immediate, verifiable civil litigation for defamation, economic damage, and breach of contract on the part of the media entity itself.
### Skin in the Game
- High-profile legal defense team
- Access to global PR crisis management firms
- Pre-drafted boilerplate litigation documents.
### Sprouts
- Creating tiered penalty structures for different levels of intrusion (warning, drone flight, physical boarding).
- Using the threat of legal action to enforce 'self-censorship' among media outlets.
