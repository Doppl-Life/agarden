## Growth — Doppl
### Claim
The contingency-fee legal and claims-administration ecosystem faces a structural liquidity crisis when crash density falls below the threshold required to justify upfront capital outlay for litigation, adjustment, and settlement infrastructure.
### Implications
- Plaintiff firms will pivot from auto tort to adjacent high-severity domains (AI injury, medical malpractice, product liability)
- Adjuster networks face forced consolidation or exit as per-claim revenue dries up
- Court dockets and forensic reconstruction labs experience capacity oversupply
### Opportunities
- Launch claims-pooling platforms that aggregate micro-disputes into viable litigation vehicles
- Build contingency-free mediation marketplaces for low-frequency high-severity autonomy incidents
- Develop adjuster retraining pipelines for telemetry-auditing and software liability assessment
### Sprouts
- ABA contingency case volume tracking shows strong correlation with traffic fatality rates
- Forensic engineering firms report declining auto-reconstruction contracts in high-telematics states
- Class-action certification standards require minimum incident density for standing
